Economic Information

Source: Study International

  • Huntington's economy has been relatively flat since 2010, with job losses in some sectors offsetting gains in others.
  • The education and healthcare sector are major economic drivers, expected to add 1,000 jobs over the next 5 years.
  • Planned natural gas and automotive manufacturing investments could boost growth.
  • The unemployment rate has hovered around 5.3% in recent quarters (Reyes et al., 2019).

Huntington is the largest city in the Cabell and Wayne counties along the Ohio River. Marshall University and healthcare facilities are major employers.

Geographic Features

Source: Twitter

  • Huntington is located in the southwestern corner of West Virginia along the Ohio River, at the confluence with the Guyandotte River.
  • The city lies within the Western Allegheny Plateau ecoregion.
  • Huntington covers 18.46 square miles, with 16.22 sq mi of land and 2.24 sq mi of water.
  • The city is divided into four main sections by the CSX railroad tracks (north-south) and First Street (east-west) (Wikipedia contributors, 2024).
