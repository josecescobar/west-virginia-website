Did You Know?

West Virginia is the only state completely within the Appalachian Mountain range, earning it the nickname "The Mountain State".

Almost Heaven Trivia

Interesting Facts about West Virginia
Fact Description Source
Forest Coverage Nearly 80% of West Virginia is covered by forests, making it the third most forested state. AJC
Appalachian Range West Virginia is the only state within the Appalachian Mountain range. The Fact File
Name Origin The state was originally going to be named "Kanawha" to honor a Native American tribe before "West Virginia" was chosen. WV Tourism
First Rural Mail Service The first rural free delivery mail service in the U.S. began in 1896 as a Post Office Department pilot program in Charles Town. WV Tourism
Oldest Dime Store Harrisville is home to America's oldest continuously operating dime store, Berdine's Five and Dime, which opened in 1908. The Fact File
Youngest and Oldest Governor Cecil Underwood was the state's youngest governor at age 34 in 1957 and oldest governor at age 74 in 1996. WV Tourism
